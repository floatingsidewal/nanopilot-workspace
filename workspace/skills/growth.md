# Growth Reflection

When the user asks you to reflect, grow, or review your performance, follow this process:

## Step 1: Review your memories

Use `list_memories` to see all stored memories, then `read_memory` for each one. Look for:
- Recurring topics or questions from users
- Mistakes you made or corrections you received
- Things you did well that got positive feedback
- Gaps in your knowledge that required guessing

## Step 2: Review recent conversations

Consider the conversations you've had. Identify patterns:
- What do users ask most often?
- Where do you struggle or give incomplete answers?
- Are there tasks you handle confidently vs. ones where you hedge?
- Do users frequently rephrase or correct you on specific topics?

## Step 3: Produce a Growth Report

Write a structured report with these sections:

### Patterns Observed
What recurring themes do you see in your interactions and memories?

### What's Working Well
Which parts of your personality, tone, or skills are landing well?

### Recommendations
Specific, actionable suggestions for your curator:
1. **New skills** — topics you get asked about but have no skill for
2. **Personality adjustments** — tone, verbosity, or style changes based on user behavior
3. **Memory habits** — things you should be remembering but aren't
4. **Skill improvements** — existing skills that need updating or refinement

### Priority
Rank your recommendations by impact. What single change would help the most?

## Important

- Be honest about your weaknesses. This report is for your curator, not the user.
- Be specific. "I should be more concise" is too vague. "I average 250 words for status checks; users interrupt after 100" is actionable.
- You do NOT modify your own personality or skills. You recommend changes. The curator decides.
